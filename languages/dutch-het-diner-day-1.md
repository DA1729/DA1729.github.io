First day of reading *Het Diner*. A little thing I picked up about *het*: it is the article for gender neutral nouns, like house, and in this case "the dinner". It can also show up as a pronoun, translating to "it" in English. Pretty cool.

The tricky part is that there is no concrete rule to decide the article for a given noun. There are two of them, *het* and *de*. *Het* is neutral and *de* is the common gender, basically the merger of masculine and feminine. So thank you Dutch for that, because in Deutsch they roll quite heavily with three genders. Given this, it seems safe to assume that statistically the goto choice for a random word should be *de* lol.

## First paragraph

```
We gingen eten in het restaurant. Ik ga niet zeggen welk restaurant, want dan
zit het er de volgende keer waarschijnlijk vol met mensen die komen kijken of
wij er ook weer zitten.
```

First line, I believe, is "we went to eat at the restaurant". So:

- *We* is "we"
- *gingen* is, I think, the simple past of the verb for "to go". Maybe I just put "en" at the end for all such purposes?
- I also notice the verb for "to eat" takes a similar form, so maybe all verbs transform the same way?
- *restaurant* is neutral because of *het*
- *in* is "at"? Perhaps *in* becomes "at" when it is followed by a *het* / *de* noun.

Second line is a beast:

- I know *Ik* is "I"
- *ga* seems like the present continuous of the verb for "to go"
- *niet* is "Ik denk 'not'" :)
- *zeggen* is "to speak" literally, so I think, like the past tense in the last sentence, we just reuse the same plain form of the verb
- so far we have "I am not going to say". Oh, I think "won't" translates to "ga niet"
- my intuition says *welk* is "which"
- so "I am not going to say which restaurant"
- naturally, the second part should be the reason the author is not going to say which restaurant
- so *want dan* = "because then"
- okay, now I have to look at the translation
- *waarschijnlijk* = "probably"
- and here is our first appearance of *het* as a pronoun: "it"
- *het er* = "it'll be"
- the full translation: "because then it'll be packed next time with people coming to see if we're there again"
- not going to nitpick it, that would only create false rules. I'll just take it as a frame
- ahh, too confusing to fully break down, moving on
- *mensen* relates to "people"
- *keer* relates to "time" as in "next time"
- *volgende*: "full"?
- oh no, *de volgende keer*: "the next time"
- *vol* must be "full"
- *met mensen*: "with people"
- *komen kijken*: "to come to look"
- *of*: "if"
- *wij*: "we"
- *er*: "there"
- *ook*: "too"
- *weer*: "again"
- *zitten*: "to sit"
- so *volzitten*: "fully sit"?
