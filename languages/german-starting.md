Second language in the section, and this time it is German. Same spirit as the Dutch project, slightly different tools.

## The method

The core bet is unchanged: absorb the language from real sentences instead of having rules handed to me up front. For German the two pillars are *Tschick* by Wolfgang Herrndorf as the novel, and Duden *Die Grammatik* as the reference grammar, the German equivalent of reading the ANS for Dutch. So one source feeds me the language as it is actually used, and the other lets me check my guesses against the standard description when an inferred rule starts to wobble.

*Tschick* is a good fit for this. It is modern, it is mostly told through a teenager's voice, and it leans heavily on dialogue, which means lots of short, real, high-frequency sentences rather than dense literary prose.

## What I expect to be different from Dutch

German is going to push back harder. Three genders instead of Dutch's two, so no statistical "just guess *de*" shortcut. Case marking that actually shows up on articles and adjectives, which Dutch mostly shed. So I expect a lot more of my early guesses to be wrong, and I expect the Duden to earn its keep faster here than the ANS did.

## Where I am right now

Day zero. *Tschick* and Duden *Die Grammatik* are on the desk. The next entries will be the actual reading: real German, real confusion, real progress.
