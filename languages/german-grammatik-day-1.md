We are studying *Die Grammatik*, which is like the german equivalent of the ANS. And guess what the first chapter is on: phonology. Now, I know where to start if I ever decide to write a grammar book.

A funny thing happened, I translated the chapter's name *Phonem und Graphem* using DeepL, and it said: 'Phoneme and Grapheme', for a moment I thought it didn't work, but Phoneme is english for the smallest and basic unit of sound in speech and Grapheme (I thought some sort of carbon polymer lol) is the smallest functional unit of a writing system. Learnt new words in both english and deutsch :p

Now the heading after that `Der Laut und die Lautstruktur des Wortes`, *und* must be and, the three weird words have der, die, des, following them so all three nouns, wortes must be words, and also note that it's des wortes, so des might be plural version of das. Die Lautstruktur, idk, sentence? Nope, the sound structure, oh ok this maps out. so structure's german must be: *struktur*, and sound perhaps *laut*? so the heading is the sound and the sound structure the words? strange, maybe des is of? sound structure of words is more natural thing to say.

Btw, I will be using the word analogue a lot, and wrote its context in een van mijn [Nederlandse blogs](https://da1729.github.io/post.html?post=dutch-ans-day-4&kind=language).

ok, so wortes is NOT plural for words, AND, something interesting and efficient is happening here, des analogues to "of the", so like of the word. cool.

The heading after it also seems to be teaching something, it's `1 Artikulation und Verschriftung der Wörter`, articulation is as no brainer as it gets, und is and. I looked it up, it's spelling, *verschriftung* and *worter* (idk how to type that double dotted o, i should probably look it up), yeah so it's words, the word (no pun intended) changed quite a bit with its plural form.

Yeah let's see one sentence, then I go to sleep. `Zu den Grundbausteinen der Sprache gehört das Wort.` *Sprache* I think is speech? No language. The analogue for the first part *Zu den Grundbausteinen* is 'the basic building block' and just ignore 'of' as one would use in english, and say zu den Grundbausteinen der Sprache (basic building block of the language is the word), so gehort, perhaps is? does map. So far, I have seen three forms of the noun 'word' itself bruhh... ok, german is weirder than dutch, anyway. Enough for today.
