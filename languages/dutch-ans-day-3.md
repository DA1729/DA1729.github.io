Neem bijvoorbeeld deze zin van de ANS:

```
Ze maken dat we bijvoorbeeld de woorden pak en bak van elkaar kunnen onderscheiden
```

Before we break it down: *zin* means sentence, so I just said "take, for example, this sentence."

So let's see. I have read somewhere that *maken* is to make, and *onderscheiden* means to differentiate. So here is a raw translation: "they make that we for example the words pak and bak from each other can differentiate."

Interesting. The natural English would be: "they make it so that, for example, we can distinguish the words pak and bak from each other."

The verb, to distinguish, came in at the very end of the second part of the sentence. And *woorden* perhaps the plural form, and *kunnen* the analogue of *can*. en ja, analoog niet vertaling.
