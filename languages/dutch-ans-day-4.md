Ok, went out for what my friends called "Friday Beer" yesterday, so two posts today (one morning, one evening).

Let's take a break from fonologie for now. let's study de zin.

*algemeen* means general. Also, I am going to use analogue and analogous rather than translation, because I feel it's a better term for these purposes. Conversion of the entire speech or sentence for the sake of communication is, I believe, what translation is. But in the case where I am breaking down all this stuff, I believe analogue holds a better place. Anyway, here are the sentences.

ok eerst deel, wat is een zin?

we hebben deze zin over zinnen :p

```
Een algemeen bevredigende definitie van de zin is moeilijk te geven.
```

*definitie* says definite, and I read *of the sentence*. *algemeen* analogues to general.

ok, time to translate:

```
It is difficult to provide a generally satisfactory definition of a sentence
```

makes sense.

last part's analogue is: difficult to give.
