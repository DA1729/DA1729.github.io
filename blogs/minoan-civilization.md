# The Minoan Civilization

Yep, we (I) are into history now. Learning languages is making me more interested in history, connect the dots yourself. Today I am studying the Minoan Civilization. All the references and links are below. Also, this is not the type of blog one would refer to, it is just me reading whatever is online and writing down whatever I find cool.

So it was apparently the "first" European civilization. It was based on the island of Crete, the most populous island of Greece, well of course it makes sense that one of the first civilizations was here. Fifth largest island in the Mediterranean Sea, the largest one being Sicily (I did not know Sicily was an island until now), anyway.

Apparently, they were connected with other cultures as well through the Aegean sea, which is one of the arms of the Mediterranean between the Greek Peninsula and Turkey.

So this archaeologist, Sir Arthur Evans, spent a lot of time and is credited with the "discovery" of this ancient civilization.

Apparently, Homer, in his poem 'The Odyssey', has also mentioned the island of Crete, as a "lovely sea-girt land."

There is this myth of King Minos, the son of Zeus and Europa. Zeus transformed himself into a white bull. Ok, wait, it is just brain rot now. And OK, we are literally on the borderline of Greek history and Greek mythology, so you get it.

I actually have some really nice theories and pictures of how myths and gods form, I will discuss them some other time, probably when I get into a discussion on such stuff with someone, because then I am quite nicely able to put all these abstract thoughts into words.

Minoans were for a long time borderline myth and history, and Thucydides, an ancient historian, such an ironic job description, described the people of Crete as historical figures.

Holy shit, I just read some ultra educated person's word, *Thalassocracy*, which means a state whose dominion, wealth, and power derive primarily from maritime supremacy and naval dominance. Insane. And it also makes sense for the Minoan civilization to be a "Thalassocracy," given that it is an island and the biggest one near Greece.

A little thing to keep in mind for me when I learn Greek, *thallas* is sea and *kratos* is power.

They obviously had their own language, which current archaeologists and historians call Linear A. Then they also found another script, Linear B, an evolution of Linear A. And B was deciphered, and is apparently the oldest Greek dialect.

Another interesting thing, they had relations with Syria, although I am not good with maps yet, I think they are quite far away in the present day.

And, as usual, we do not really know how it ended. Some suggested the volcano on *Thera* ended it, BUT, the geography does not really check out with the theory, although some theories do suggest some sort of economic and trade collapse, but not too severe to end a civilization.
