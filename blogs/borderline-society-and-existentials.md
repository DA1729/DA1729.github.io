And now that I am reading a lot, everywhere, language books, novels, stuff for the things I am working on, I feel like writing even more. Also, the project that I am working on: I am NOT going to reveal what I am working on. What I expected to be very high level, applied, systems work has turned into something, well, honestly, something that I always wanted to pursue, and a few months back I literally declared it to be the "meta-purpose" of me as a human being. And I am standing strong still, mostly because I am being paid and have been given the best working space, and OMG ESAT KU Leuven, AMAZING department, and COSIC, well, LEGENDARY.

Based off my profile, before yall assume that I must have settled on some really lame purpose, something related to having absolute privacy and all that. Ok, homomorphic encryption is still the coolest shit ever and one of the best things one has done with mathematics (not saying THE, before yall start naming Euler), but yeah, the thing that I am working on, and atp going to be working on for years, is something that put me into some existential crisis during my mid-sems, and that DID affect my performance a lot, but idc.

And I am literally naming it my purpose, because it feels like a really stable yet subtle ground between society and "existentials". yeah, I will coin new terms.

The fact is, everyone knows when is the end. Researchers are just advancing physics, building abstraction layers in mathematics, and all that. Everyone knows that the human species will literally vanish without knowing anything. It's either that, or we all vanish the moment we uncover the truth. But since I have a habit of applying half baked intuitions of physics theorems to everything: since simultaneous events are impossible (special relativity), assuming my hypothesis is true, the moment we uncover everything, the vanishing must happen simultaneously, which is impossible, hence a contradiction. Now, I cannot just go ahead and imply we will never uncover everything. I try my best to stay away from half baked logic. Logical intuition, yes; half baked, no. At least now, earlier I have done a lot.

Yeah, so I know that, at least I have convinced myself, that we shall never uncover the secret, but we can't stop either, because everything we do as a society, at the end, collectively, is motivated towards understanding the universe. And think about it for a second. Maps and borders have changed, languages have changed, types of jobs have changed, but one thing has remained constant throughout, and that is the fact that we don't know why we are here and we want to know why we are here. They say that the primary purpose of a species is to keep reproducing. NO. Reproduction is a consequence of something even more fundamental, and that is to understand nature, the universe.

And this argument even leads to some very interesting consequences, that make sense. I don't know whether anyone has wondered this, I definitely have, and that is: why do we even co-operate with each other? If we were to be inherently greedy and all that, we would be living in a "society" where everyone is trying to kill each other actively. Not even animals, it's something even more raw, and well, it wouldn't exist. So in a way, apart from all the bad that has happened, and that is happening, there is still a sub-set, and I don't know what will happen in the next 100 years, but let's just agree that we all have a hunch saying that humanity is not ending anytime soon. And that's because we want to understand the universe we are in.

Ok, now my eyes are like hurting, very long day today. I will be writing a lot from now, and I will give yall this, that writing such stuff will literally help me with the project that I am working on. so yeah, borderline society and existentials.
